Sandy Springs North End Design

Problem & Solution

Four 10-acre aging shopping centers in the North End of Sandy Springs are experiencing redevelopment pressure: North River Village, River Springs, Northridge, and North Springs Centers. The City of Sandy Springs wanted to study 3 versions of concept plans and their financial feasibility for each of the sites. However, the success of any project here depends on making Roswell Road more walkable.

A process driven by public input resulted in a report documenting the entire process, 13 site plans, financial feasibility, and recommendations.

Team Members: TSW (project management, site design, public engagement), KB Advisory Group (market analysis and financial feasibility), Long Engineering (stormwater management and infrastructure)

20 Participant-led Discussion Groups of 10-12 people helped us define goals and desires through curated activities

Engaging the community during the pandemic


We held an in-person kick-off meeting before the pandemic began with approximately 200 participants who participated in small discussion groups to ensure each person had a voice in the process. They shared their results using a polling software, which posted the results live on a large screen, allowing them to see what others were saying.

Then the world stopped, and we re-calibrated.

We moved to an entirely virtual engagement input process with online dating profiles for specific land uses, interactive maps showing in-progress designs, surveys, and online meetings. Participation remained steady throughout 2020.

“The North End of Sandy Springs will redevelop in a way the fulfills existing and future demand for housing, commercial space, and amenities that can attract a variety of residents and patrons from all backgrounds and lifestyles.”

Community values
emerged as five key goals:

1. Ensure a variety of housing options to accommodate all types of residents.

2. Model how mixed-use (retail, office, housing, an institutional uses, and green space) environments can work in Sandy Springs.

3. Build upon existing green spaces and parks to create a cohesive public space network.

4. Improve multi-modal connectivity throughout the North End, starting with the four sites and Roswell Road.

5. Attract and support local small businesses in the North End.

Connecting to the Chattahoochee, providing places to live,
and re-envisioning the North End.


Based on public input and financial analysis, site plans were revised multiple times. In the final versions, more and larger green spaces were added, building heights were reduced on some concepts for North Springs and River Springs Centers, and we reiterated the need for City investment in housing and public infrastructure to make some redevelopment work financially.

Balancing financial feasibility
and community values.



The final recommendations provide guidance on policies and funding mechanisms the City of Sandy Springs can use to incentivize redevelopment of these sites that provides what the community values: affordable housing, greenspace, and connectivity.

What happens now?

The City is updating their zoning code based on the recommendations of this plan to be more flexible and allow more housing types. Developers may be more inclined to approach the City with projects because a plan is in place.
